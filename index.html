Electro-THermal mOdel for State estimation

A three-stage cascaded LSTM framework that closes the electro-thermal feedback loop in Li-ion battery management—co-estimating SOC and surface temperature without auxiliary thermal sensors.

// 01 — Motivation

The Open Loop Problem

Current BMS architectures treat SOC and surface temperature as decoupled estimates, ignoring the non-linear electro-thermal feedback that governs Li-ion cell performance.

When thermal-electrochemical coupling goes unmodeled, thermal runaway risk increases substantially. Prior data-driven approaches either treat temperature as a measured input or require auxiliary sensors.

⚡ Research Gap

The electro-thermal feedback loop remains open. ETHOS closes it with a sensor-free, physics-ordered cascade.

01
SOC and temperature treated as decoupled estimates in conventional BMS
02
Non-linear electro-thermal feedback ignored, raising thermal runaway risk
03
Existing models require auxiliary temperature sensor or treat T as a given input
04
No prior work enforces physics-consistent ordering: I → V → T → SOC
